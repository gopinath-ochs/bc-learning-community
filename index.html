Read on

Bhaktivedanta College is a community of devotional scholarship rooted in the Gaudiya Vaishnava tradition, offering tutorial-led courses, retreats, and guided study with established scholars.

The idea

We are exploring the creation of a Learning Community: a shared space for students and scholars to stay connected, continue learning, and grow together outside of formal coursework.

Think of it as the College campus, but virtual. A place where you belong, where inquiry continues, and where your relationship with the tradition and with fellow students deepens over time.

This would be a low-cost monthly membership, something in the region of €10 to €15 per month, distinct from individual courses, which would continue to be offered and priced separately.

Why we're exploring this

You've taken a course, or attended a retreat, or spent time in dialogue with a scholar who opened something up for you. And then it ends. The connection fades. You're left waiting for the next offering, wondering when you'll have that experience again.

That's the gap we want to close.

Bhaktivedanta College is moving toward smaller, more intimate cohorts, with a teacher to student ratio of about 1:10. That allows for the kind of guided dialogue and personal engagement that makes this education genuinely transformative. But it also means fewer students can participate in any given course.

The Learning Community would ensure that your engagement with the College doesn't have to stop between courses. Ongoing relationships with scholars. Peer conversations. A shared intellectual and devotional life. Not just courses, but a community you belong to.

What membership might include

These are the kinds of offerings we're considering. Nothing is finalised, and your feedback will shape what we actually build.

Scholar Q&A

Monthly live sessions where you can put your questions directly to the scholars. No agenda, no formality. Just ask them anything.

Spotlight Series

A monthly presentation by a scholar sharing their latest research, thinking, or reflections on a topic within the tradition.

Reading Groups

Thematic study circles where students read and discuss texts together, guided by shared curiosity and mutual inquiry.

Scholarly Library

Access to a curated collection of books, articles, and recordings that may be difficult to find elsewhere.

Student Forums

A space for peer dialogue, questions, and shared discoveries. The kind of conversation that sustains a learning community.

Retreat Access

Early access and priority booking for College retreats and residential programmes as they become available.

The final shape of the community will be informed by your feedback below.
